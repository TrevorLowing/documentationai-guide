## Overview

RMF Coach simulates the complete NIST Risk Management Framework process for FedRAMP Moderate authorizations. You work with real NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 controls, build implementation narratives, and make authorization decisions that mirror live federal cloud projects.

## NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 Control Families

The simulation uses the same control families you encounter in actual FedRAMP packages. Each family contains controls that address specific security and privacy requirements.

## Customer vs. CSP Responsibility

Understanding inheritance is critical. You must correctly identify which controls your organization owns, which the cloud service provider manages, and which are fully inherited.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Customer Owned" icon="user">
    These controls require you to write SSP implementation narratives. Examples include AC-2 Account Management and AU-6 Audit Review.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="CSP Shared" icon="cloud">
    Controls where both parties share responsibility. You document the customer portion while referencing the provider's FedRAMP authorization.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Fully Inherited" icon="arrow-down">
    Controls you inherit entirely from the CSP. No implementation narrative is required, but you must still verify the inheritance path.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## SSP Implementation Narratives

Your SSP narratives must meet quality gates before advancing. The simulation evaluates narrative completeness, control applicability, and alignment with the Customer Responsibility Matrix.

<Callout kind="info">
  Strong narratives include specific implementation details, responsible roles, and evidence artifacts rather than generic policy statements.
</Callout>

## POA&M, Findings, and Authorization Decisions

After assessment, you manage Plan of Action and Milestones entries for any findings.

<Expandable title="How POA&M entries are created" default-open="false">
  The simulation generates findings based on your control responses. You then create POA&M items with scheduled completion dates, resources required, and milestones. Authorization decisions depend on the severity and remediation timeline of open items.
</Expandable>
