## Overview

RMF Coach guides you through a complete FedRAMP Moderate authorization cycle. You configure a cloud system, select controls, write implementation narratives, respond to evidence probes, and finalize an authorization decision. The platform mirrors real NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 workflows so your decisions translate directly to live engagements.

<Callout kind="info">
  Always review the system primer before selecting controls. Inheritance decisions made here affect the entire Customer Responsibility Matrix.
</Callout>

## Phase 1: Configure Your System

Begin by selecting a deployment scenario and reviewing inherited controls.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose scenario" icon="cloud">
    Select a cloud provider and baseline. The platform loads a pre-populated system security plan with 120+ controls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the CRM" icon="list">
    Examine the Customer Responsibility Matrix. Identify controls your organization owns versus those inherited from the CSP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust boundaries" icon="settings">
    Update system boundaries and data flows. Changes automatically propagate to control applicability.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Selecting Implementation Narratives

Strong narratives describe how each control is implemented, not just restate requirements.

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="AC-2 Account Management" icon="users" href="#narratives">
    Define procedures for account provisioning, periodic review, and revocation within 24 hours of termination.
  </Card>
  <Card title="AU-2 Audit Events" icon="file-text" href="#narratives">
    Specify which events are logged, retention periods, and integration with your SIEM solution.
  </Card>
</Columns>

<Tabs>

    The organization provisions privileged accounts through a formal access request workflow in ServiceNow. Requests require manager approval and ISSO review. ## Working Through Evidence Probes

After narrative submission, the platform generates evidence probes and method questions.

<Expandable title="Sample probe: AC-2(1)" default-open="false">
  The assessor asks: "Provide the last quarterly account review report and evidence of automated revocation for terminated users."

  Respond with:
  - CSV export from the identity system
  - Screenshot of the HR integration dashboard
  - Policy reference showing the 24-hour revocation SLA
</Expandable>

## Updating the POA&M and Authorization

Track findings in the Plan of Action and Milestones. When all high-risk items reach "completed" status, you submit for authorization.

```json
{
  "poam_item": "AC-2-001",
  "weakness": "Quarterly review process not documented",
  "status": "in-progress",
  "scheduled_completion": "2025-03-15",
  "milestone": "Draft procedure and obtain ISSO approval"
}
```

Once the POA&M is current, the Authorizing Official reviews the package. You receive one of three decisions: authorized, authorized with conditions, or denied. The simulation logs every decision for after-action review.
